Ruth Bader Ginsburg

The late Supreme Court Justice, Ruth Bader Ginsburg.

Roles

Roles Ruth Bader Ginsburg held throughout her life.

  1. Associate Justice of the Supreme Court

    In office from 1993 to 2020
    • Second woman to serve on the US Supreme Court
    • Wrote the majority opinion challenging the Virginia Military Institute's admissions process (US v. Virgina, 1996)
    • Wrote the majority opinion in Olmstead v. L.C, 1999. Provided an important victory to people with disabilities

  2. Founder of the ACLU's Women's Rights Project

    Argued 6 gender discrimination cases before the Supreme Court, won 5
    • Set the basis against sex discrimination (Frontiero v. Richardson, 1973)
    • Argued against the Social Security Act's gender-based distinction regarding survivor benefits (Califano v. Goldfarb, 1977)
    • Argued against a Louisiana law that extempted women from serving on juries unless they filed a written declaration (Edwards v. Healy, 1975)

  3. Law Professor

    Columbia Univeristy School of Law and Rutgers University
    • Co-authored the first law school casebook on sex discrimination
    • Became first tenured woman at Columbia
    • Co-founded the first law journal in the U.S to focus on women's rights
